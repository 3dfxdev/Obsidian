OBLIGE : Recent Development

by Andrew Apted.   22nd November 2008

 

 

Progress on OBLIGE has been slow, some might say [i]glacial[/i], since the last update, but things are still happening.

There was several attempts at creating better whole-level designs for maps. The first one was called a "Zone" system, big square areas that would be populated with rooms, or sub-divided into smaller zones. Seemed promising but it didn't work out. Next I tried a "Room Fab" system, which connected pre-fabricated room shapes together to make a whole map. It was great at making room/hallway mazes, but lousy when it came to outdoor areas. The final system is called the "Land Map", it involves making a crude representation which consists of building, ground or liquid areas. This one is working very nicely.

The GUI has changed again, especially the area for Custom Modules. Getting the custom modules to work properly (including module-specific options) and getting CONFIG.CFG loading/saving again all took a long time, but is working fine now.

The Quest system, which is responsible for deciding where to put locked doors and what keys/switches to use for them, needed to be rewritten. The old way decided all the quests first, and creating rooms to fulfil those quests. That didn't work with the Land-Map system, hence the new way firstly connects all the rooms (including outdoor areas), then decides how to split the level into smaller quests and where the locked doors should go. It's actually harder than it sounds.
